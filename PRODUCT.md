# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Beginner and casual guitarists tuning at home, typically on mobile. They open the app with one task in mind — get in tune — and close it the moment they're done. They may not know what "cents" means. The UI must answer "am I in tune?" before they have to form the question.

## Product Purpose

EasyGuitarTuner is a chromatic tuner for guitar (6-string, 12-string) and ukulele. It detects pitch via the device microphone and shows the player whether each string is flat, sharp, or in tune. Success is a guitarist who opens the app, tunes their strings in under a minute, and leaves without confusion.

## Brand Personality

Precise · Clean · Trusted

The tuner should feel like a high-quality physical instrument — not software, not an app, not a platform. The aesthetic is calibrated and scientific: dark, minimal, exact. A beginner should feel confident picking it up; an experienced player should trust the readings.

## Anti-references

Not a SaaS dashboard. No metric cards, no sidebar navigation, no "platform" chrome. The app does one thing. It should feel nothing like a web product with a marketing layer grafted on.

Also avoid: skeuomorphic tuner UIs with fake analog gauges and busy backgrounds; gamified apps with confetti or achievement badges; anything that distracts from the pitch signal.

## Design Principles

1. **Signal over chrome** — the pitch reading is the product. Every UI element earns its space or disappears. Decoration is a cost.
2. **Instant confidence** — a beginner's first question is "am I in tune?" Answer it before they form the question. No loading states, no setup friction, no cognitive overhead.
3. **Instrument, not software** — visual language should feel calibrated and physical, not web-app-y. Dark theme, precise typography, restrained palette.
4. **One thing at once** — the app has one task. The layout should make it impossible to feel lost or overwhelmed. No competing calls to action.
5. **Earned trust through accuracy** — colors, motion, and feedback must be truthful. Green means in tune. The indicator must never flicker or mislead.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Best effort. No formal WCAG level required, but body text must remain legible and interactive elements must have accessible labels. Color alone should never be the sole signal for tuning state.
